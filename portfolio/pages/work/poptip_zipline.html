Overview

Any given day, million upon millions of tweets come in from millions of creators. You set up a Zipline of classifiers – handles, hashtags, words, phrases, common names, proper names. We’ll look at the classifiers and we’ll look at the phrases, and we’ll show you what’s relevant to you.

The Problem

A lot of what we see from brands is pushing content as a means to drive engagement. What that results in are things like retweets, likes and shares, all of which are valuable but missing out on the key engagement that can happen with social content: discussion.

The problem is that there is so much content, let’s narrow it down. It’s sifting through the noise.

Poptip Zipline

Zipline was a market research tool that allowed users to track social media crowd reaction through tracking text and images. The biggest challenge of designing this prodcut was making sense out of all the noise and vastness of social media data.

Zipline Detail View Live

Emphasis on Real-Time

How fast does your Facebook timeline move? How about your Twitter feed? Social doesn’t stop. There is a constant flow of new information, discussion and opinion and we can’t sit back and miss out on it. Understanding what people are saying in the moment is incredible important as it may impact that next ad buy your brand makes, or the content that you decide to publish. Real-time allows you to stay relevant and not fall behind the crowd in the conversation. If anything, you can stay ahead and own the conversation. The Product

When a user clicks on the Zipline, one goes into the detail view. The main goal of the detail view was to allow users to see as much data possible in a single view while keeping the inflow of data as less visually jarring as possible.
