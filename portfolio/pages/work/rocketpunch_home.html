RocketPunch Home

The one stop business feed for your professional network

Overview

The objective of the project was to redesign the RocketPunch homepage to reflect our main mission: encouraging people to form connections and discover useful content. There were many iterations which involved a design sprint failure, but through those trials and errors we were able to reach a final solution: a timeline formed by your professional network.

The business feed allows our users to enjoy relevent content to be automatically pushed.

Lead Product Designer

Research, Information Architecture, Interaction, UI/UX Design & testing

Launched in 2018

The Problem

The problem was obvious. Our users were barely using our homepage. Data showed that the typical user flow when someone lands on our homepage was to go straight to our secondary pages such as search pages or profiles.

Our homepage was adding no value for our users.

Despite our original intent of fostering content discovery, the home page was serving no functionality aside from being there for the sake of being there.

Kicking off a Design Sprint (That failed)

We knew we had to redesign the homepage, but there were so much uncertainty. Rocketpunch was going through a lot changes as a company in 2018. The company started off as a jobs/recruitment platform but we wanted to further pursue our mission of helping people succeed in their career by adding in personal and corporate profiles.

Day #2 into our design sprint
The company was at a stage where it was necessary for us to reassess our product goals.

As a team we decided that running a week long design sprint would be the best way to target this major problem surrounded with so much ambiguity.

User Research

After identifying our target user group we took a deeper look into the daily routines of the accounts that our target. Here are a few examples.

Figuring Out the Right Target Users

These were the users who had the strong desire to communicate with their audience, yet had the most difficulty in finding an effective way to listen to their audience due to the sheer number of reactions they receive.

Identifying Pain Points

1. Too many reactions from audience to individually go through.
2. Lack of single app to manage identical posts across multiple platforms
3. Lack of ways to filter or analyze crowd reactions

These are quite unique problems specific to power users, but we learned through our user interviews that these were real problems that were causing much inconvenience. For instance, one of our avid users who was a sports broadcaster would post a question each morning and would have his assistant manually count the comments and record the results on an excel sheet. Who would've thought people would be doing this in the 21st century...

The Solution

Poptip Questions provided our target users a solution for the three major pain points through automization and providing a single platform that allows you to manage mutiple social media platforms. We were able to create a userflow that minimized the manual labor and redundancy. Below is how we were able to change our users' experience of asking a question on Twitter or Facebook.

Project Takeaway

Poptip Questions was Poptip's very first product as well as the very first product I ever designed. Thus, every single step was a valuable takeaway. However, in hindsight as an experienced product designer I see that it was a unique opportunity to work with dynamic data. Because the data flow was constant yet unpredictable it was very important to design for unforseen edge cases.

Shoot for Simplicity and Clarity

Simplicity and clarity are the best ways to avoid mishappenings. Whether it be content or visual embelishment, Poptip Questions is the product that made me develop a habit of questioning myself whether an element is truely necessary, which also comes with the benifit of lowering engineering cost.
